FLUX #011 - Street Directory

20 March 2020 (First published on 8kguy.com)

Google Maps is powerful, but it lacks a few features, like checking for multistory car parks. Thus, you might have installed another app for that, like Street Directory. But anyone that used Street Directory’s app knows how bad it is. In this FLUX, let’s see how we can fix that.

Issue #1: Intrusive Ads

Street Directory is a business, so having ads in their app is unavoidable. However, what could have been avoided is how intrusive the ads are. Open the app and the first thing you see is a full-screen ad. Tap on “Home”? Full-screen ad. Checking for offline maps? Another full-screen ad.

Issue #2: Ridiculously Small Toggles

On a few of the pages, toggles are tiny and cluttered. Just look at how close up those expressway toggles are next to each other. Not only is it hard to read, it is easy to tap on the wrong thing. With so much available space on screen, they could have easily made these toggles larger.

Issue #3: Unnecessary Hamburger Menu

There is a hamburger menu, but most of its content can be accessed from the menu bar below. The others are less used stuff. In other words, this app doesn’t need the hamburger menu.

Issue #4: Too Much Distraction

Compared to Google Maps, there is a lot of colors on map. While this allows for more detailed labeling of buildings, some of the colors are just too distracting. For instance, look at the map below; odds are, the first color that catches your attention is that emerald green signifying a garden.

While I would really like to address this in my concept, it just takes too much time to redo the map. Street Directory, if you are reading this, please do something about it.

Issue #5: Severe Lack of Polishing

On the map, you can pinch in/out to zoom, so why is there a need for the -/+ toggles? Also, the traffic alert feature is not yet available. Having its page in the app right now just adds to the clutter.

Every section of the directory has these large, ugly boxes used as dividers. Since there are already borders though, there is no reason to have it. Also, photos for many of the listings look unprofessional. They may not be added by Street Directory, but still, it degrades the experience.

Concept by 8K

Standardized Banner Ads

Banner ads are a lot less intrusive than full-screen ads. Rather than having a mix of ad types, I recommend just having a banner ad on every single page. The banner is located at the bottom of most pages, except for the map. To make the search bar reachable with one hand, I had to shift it to the bottom.

Simplified Map Experience

There is no “Directions” in the new menu. As it works hand-in-hand with the map, merging them creates a more seamless experience. Simply search your destination and click on the navigation icon. By default, starting point is your current location, but you can change it by tapping on the search bar.

Before tapping “Go”, you can tap on the icon directly above to change your mode of transportation. A pop-up appears and you can compare duration and price from here. The app remembers your last selection.

When searching up a location, you can tap on the star icon to favorite it. You can then see the list of your favorite locations by tapping on the search bar.

Large Buttons Traffic Page

I enlarged the expressway toggles to fill the whole page. While this adds an extra step for users of the default option (causeway), it makes things a lot easier for users of the other 7 options.

On the page showing traffic, I reduced the size of images, to fit two images per row. This decreases the need for scrolling by up to 3x. Seeing fine details is harder, but that is not the purpose of these images. As long as the user can tell if traffic is heavy from the images, it achieves its goal.

Curated Directory

The current categories for the directory doesn’t make a lot of sense, so I made new ones. Also, instead of showing the image of the first entry, I opted for a generic image that represents the category.

An issue I had with the directory is how unprofessional listing images look. With a mix of logos, food images and store images, it is a mess. To address this, Street Directory can standardize what they want for the image. For example, in this concept, every listing has an image of inside the store.

When you click on the listing, there are now 2 icons at the bottom: one to call and the other to navigate. With the changes, it is easier to reach them, and you are less likely to click on the wrong one.

Settings

You might have noticed that the nearby page is gone. Its function overlaps with the search bar’s, and it would be better to just have one solution. The settings page contains all the miscellaneous stuff.
