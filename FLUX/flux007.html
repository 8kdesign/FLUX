FLUX #007 - SXFI AIR Control

31 January 2020 (First published on 8kguy.com)

Before I went on a hiatus last year, I reviewed a pair of headphones called SXFI Air. It boasts Creative’s SXFI technology, which makes any audio come alive with holography. To use it, you need to install 2 apps: SXFI app for ear mapping, and SXFI AIR Control app for control over the lights.

Is there a need for 2 apps? Can’t we merge them into one app? In this FLUX, let’s see how we can create an app that betters experience for SXFI headphone owners.

Issue #1: Unnecessary Hamburger Menu

The SXFI Control app has too many ways to do the same thing. If half of the menu options can be accessed through the dashboard, what is the point of the menu? Also, the other half are stuff users won’t touch often, if at all. This pretty much mean that the menu is completely useless.

Issue #2: Semi-Music Player App

With a music control bar at the bottom, you can skip to the pause or next track from SXFI AIR Control. However, you can’t select what songs to play, unless the source is SD card. What it actually does is tap on your phone’s default music app, so it is not a true music player app.

But why should you have 2 apps running when listing to music? You can turn on/off SXFI through the headphones. You can’t change the light’s color, but how often will you need to do that? That makes SXFI AIR Control rather redundant. In the end, users will just forget about the app.

Issue #3: Requires Separate App for Mapping

As mentioned at the start, you need to install a seperate app to create a SXFI profile. Maybe Creative felt that developing the mapping feature on 2 apps isn’t efficient. Then why have 2 apps in the first place?

Breaking down the features in both apps, you will see many overlapping features. It is possible to merge them together. Having it as just one app simplifies things and reduces confusion for users.

Concept by 8K

Music Selection From Dashboard

Toggles on the original dashboard were taking up too much space, so I reduced their size. This allowed for a lot more content without scrolling. Since we want this to be a music app, I added song list to the remaining space, keeping the control bar. All commonly used toggles can be accessed here.

After using Sennheiser’s CapTune app, I realize that choosing tracks from a list so small can be annoying. Hence, there should be a way to make it longer if the user wants to. By swiping up from the category selection, users can make the music list fill up the entire screen.

Feature Popups

Tapping on the light SXFI and EQ toggles will open a pop-up, where you can turn it on/off or change its settings. I opted for this over a new page as it makes the app feel lighter. Holding on the toggles will let you quickly toggle it on/off. HEX selection for light colour has been removed for a simpler experience.

From the SXFI popup, you add new SXFI profiles, directly from the app.

Header Drop-Down

The header on top shows you the connected device, as well as its battery status. Tapping on it reveals more options. This is also where you can add and connect to more devices.

You will see information only relevant to your connected device here. For the SXFI AMP, here is where you select headphones. And when you return to the home page, you will see that the toggles have been updated to only show what is relevant. No selection of audio source or light color.

Lastly, we have the settings page, which is where all the chapalang is placed.
