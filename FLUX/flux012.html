FLUX #012 - Old Change Kee

27 March 2020 (First published on 8kguy.com)

This FLUX is a little different from the usual. Instead of an app, we will be working on Old Chang Kee website. It isn’t a website that people visit often, which might explain why its design seems so dated.

Issue #1: Not Mobile Friendly

The website isn’t optimized for smartphones, so what you see on your desktop is what you see on your phone. With phones being our primary means of searching the web today, that isn’t ideal.

Issue #2: Too Many Menus That Do the Same Thing

From the home page, you can see 3 menus: the main one on top, one on the side and one in the footer. And there are common items in all of them. Having one on top would have been good enough.

In the footer menu, you have an option to view the sitemap, something users don’t need to see. There is also a Google Forms survey that no longer accept entries. Why is this still here?

Issue #3: Pointless Home Page

A good home page is one that provides users with important information at first glance. This is not one of them. While you do get a pop-up for a promo when you load the page, the rest of the home page is unhelpful. I get that they love their photos, but the large slideshow banner doesn’t tell the user anything.

What about the video? From the shortened title, users can’t tell what it is about. And the small size and lower-screen position only makes it harder for the video to get a user’s interest.

Issue #4: Buttons That Don’t Work

See the like button on top? That isn’t a real like button. Instead, it redirects you to Old Chang Kee’s Facebook Page. On the “Current News & Promotions” page, the drop-down arrow is also fake. It isn’t even a button. To open it, you have to click on “view”. Why you gotta play with my heart?

Issue #5: Incoherent & Repetitive Contacts Page

I have never seen a contacts page as confusing as this. First, we have a banner with contact number for career queries. Next, we have a chunk of text with the email for general inquiries. To the right, we have more info for general inquiries. The same email, plus a phone number stated twice. What can I say? It is a mess…

A More Useful Home Screen

There are two paths to adding content to the home screen. The first is to figure out what users want when they visit Old Chang Kee’s website. The second path is to add what Old Chang Kee wants users to see.

Unfortunately, there are just too many reasons why users would do that, and none of them seems more likely than the others. They might be here to find out more about Old Chang Kee’s products. They could also be searching for an outlet near them. Or maybe they want to find out more about catering services.

We can’t add all of that on one page, or it will be a very long page. Hence, I chose to approach this via the second path. The idea of showing users the latest promotion is good, but it would be better if it wasn’t a pop-up. So I added it as an element of the home page. And added more.

Originally, I wanted to make it a banner slideshow, However, all of the promotional images are square. Cropping it cuts out important information, but having it whole takes up too much space. My solution: place a few of them together, than reduce the quantity on smaller screens.

With plenty of space remaining, I merged the products page with the home page. Instead of having a page for each product, I made all of it visible on a single page.

Revising Menu Options

With promotions and products on the home page, we can remove the “Current News & Promotions” and “products” page. The original menu bar missed out the “Catering Service” page, so I added it in.

A Neater Content & Franchise Page

I simplified the content page such that no content is repeated and it is easy to find what you need.

For coherence, I adopted a similar layout for the franchise page. I felt that requirements and contact are more important than the benefits, so I placed them on the left, where users are likely to read first.

Simplified Design Overall

I applied the same design style across all the other pages. Check them out here if you haven’t already. As catering services and investor relations are hosted seperately, I left them out.
