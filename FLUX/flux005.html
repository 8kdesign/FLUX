FLUX #005 - VoicePing

13 December 2019 (First published on 8kguy.com)

If you recall, I reviewed a pair of Smart Walkies half a year ago. For those new to them, they are basically phones that run a walkie talkie app called VoicePing. The app works, but it isn’t very intuitive. And if you are a business hoping to adopt it, this can be a problem. So in this FLUX, let’s see how we can fix that.

A Quick Intro to VoicePing

You will need to understand how this app works before you will understand its issues. It is sort of like WhatsApp, where you can chat in groups or privately. Unlike WhatsApp though, the app will read out any incoming messages. Also, with a push-to-talk button, you can quickly talk to someone on the other side.

But which channel do you talk to when you press the button? That is where the default channel comes into play. When someone sends you a message, you automatically switch to their channel. But if you do not respond after a few seconds, the app will switch back to your default channel.

Issue #1: Lack of Labeling

Clear labels help reduce the learning curve of apps, and is something that VoicePing can benefit from. Just take a look at the main “recent” page. Are you able to tell what it means for these circles to be here? Why is this circle bigger than others? Anyone new to the app will not know.

Next, I talked about default channels, but how do you tell which channel it is? Well, it is the first app on the list on page 2, provided you have a default channel. But as a new user, how would you know?

Issue #2: Too Many Steps to Mute & Set Default

Setting your default channel takes four steps, when it could easily be done in less. First, you need to select the channel. Then, tap on the header, before selecting “Set as Default Channel”. Lastly, select “Yes”. The more steps there are. the less likely users are going to use it.

Muting channels is equally as complicated. If you are doing something important on one channel and do not wish to be interrupted, you will have to repeat this step for every other channel.

Issue #3: Cluttered Settings

VoicePing’s settings page is a long list. While it is well labeled, scrolling through it is equivalent to reading through 3 full pages of content. Not easy for the user to find what they need. Also, the default settings and clear all settings buttons are too close to the exit button for anyone’s comfort.

Concept by 8K

An Unambiguous Main Page

Addressing the first issue, I added labels to the main page. On top of showing the current and recent channels, we also have the locked channel on top. Additionally, I added a quick talk button beside the current channel, for users who do not have the push-to-talk button.

Channels under the recent will no longer vanish after you read them. Instead, to show unread channels, I added a ring outside each icon. This ring turns green when there is an unread message.

Next, let’s look at the page when you swipe left. The channel list now has a filter, to sort the channels into groups and individuals. This will make searching easier. There is also an option to show only unread channels. No matter which tab you are on though, the locked channel is shown on top.

New Shortcuts for Greater Convenience

On the original app, you can hold down on a channel to show shortcuts for recording and playing messages. So I thought: Why not include a mute and lock channel shortcut here? This will greatly reduce the number of steps required to lock and mute any channel.

Next, I revamped the volume shortcut below. Instead of alternating between 50% and 100% volume, you now have 3 options: play all, mute all or mute all except locked channel. If you want to focus only on one channel, you no longer need to mute all other channels one at a time.

Reorganized Settings

There are 5 categories in the new settings, plus a feedback form link. This feedback form was previously outside of the settings. And in each category, the settings are further sorted into subcategories. I hid the “default settings” and “clear all messages” buttons under the “About App” page.
