FLUX #008 - NTUC Foodfare

14 February 2020 (First published on 8kguy.com)

In recent years, NTUC expanded their Foodfare portfolio with hawker centres. In an attempt to keep up with trends, Foodfare created their own app. With this app, you can pay for meals with your phone at 2 of their 3 hawker centres. And that is all it does. And setting it up is a painful process.

Shouldn’t mobile payment make things more convenient? Why use this crappy app when they already have a card payment solution that works so much better? So in this FLUX, let’s take a look at how we can improve the NTUC Foodfare app, and justify its existence.

Issue #1: Unpleasant Setup Process

One thing that irks me is that the app doesn’t prompt you to register an account. Instead, you have to tap once to select a hawker centre, then tap again on “Plus! Pay” to go to the register or login page. Why so troublesome? You will then be required to use your phone number to login.

If you are a new user, the app then prompts you to fill in more information. The first entry box says “only for physical card”. Are users suppose to guess that they should enter their card number there? Submit and the app goes back to the phone number entry page. Why is there a need for this additional step?

After click “verify” and doing 2FA, you are required to enter a 4 digit password. Here is where I met with another issue. The UI doesn’t respond when you type in the numbers, though it is registering your input in the background. This creates a lot of confusion for the user.

Issue #2: Redundancy

As an alternative to Plus! Pay, the app also has a toggle for PayLah , which opens the PayLah app. But if you are going to use PayLah, why open it through the Foodfare app? Also, why should anyone use Plus! Pay if they can use PayLah, which is available almost everywhere?

This leads me to question: Is Foodfare serious about Plus! Pay as a mobile payment option, or are they doing it just to be trendy? A lot of companies in Singapore are using tech for the sake of it, not because it can improve convenience or efficiency. With that mindset, all of this is just a waste of money.

Issue #3: Extra Steps

In multiple cases, users have to do more tapping then necessary. For example, before you can use Plus! Pay, you have to select a hawker centre. Do the hawker centre I am in even affect how Plus! Pay works? I doubt so. If it does, what is preventing a unified payment system?

On the top up page, there is a “Foodfare Cash Kiosk” toggle, which opens a page asking you to go to a kiosk when clicked. Why is there a need for a toggle? Why not just show the message on the top up page?

Also, tapping on the “stalls” tab doesn’t immediately show you the stall list. You have to tap on “hawker stalls” to view the list. Speaking of the list, what can users gain from it? It doesn’t show what the store sell, nor where exactly it is. Store numbers are meaningless without a map.

Concept by 8K

Login & Registration

All apps that require an account should start from the login page. So that was where I started this concept from. I renamed the “verify” to “login / register” to reduce confusion. 2FA is carried out for existing users, while new users are asked to fill in their particulars.

In the concept, the physical card number box is hidden for users that do not click the “I have a physical Plus card” button. I also brought the 4 digit password into this form, to simplify the registration process. After filling out the form and 2FA, users can immediately use the app, without having to sign in.

Hawker Centre Guide

With access to the GPS, we can detect which hawker centre the user is at. This negates the need for selecting of hawker centre, simplifying the process of using the app. But in the event that the GPS isn’t working, users will be prompted to manually select the hawker centre.

How can we add value to this app, to make it worth downloading? How do we make the shop list into something more useful? My first step was to add a map of the hawker centre.

Originally, I wanted the map to show where the user is. However, due to GPS limitation, we are unable to get the exact position of the user. But we do know the direction the user is facing, so the map will at least be able to turn to where the user is facing. More about this map in a while.

Tapping on the toggles below will show the drink store, tray return, toilet and lifts on the map.

Deciding what to eat can be a huge headache, so I decided to turn the stores list into a “What to Eat” generator. It adds fun to the app, and helps to recommend stores that users may not have considered eating. For Muslims and vegetarians, there are separate toggles for the random store generator.

A pop-up will appear, with an image of the store and opening hours. Here, you get 2 toggles. “See Menu” shows the store’s menu if available, and “Show on Map” will show the store’s position on the map. Not interested in the store? Click on the arrows below to see another store.

Payment & Top Up

We want users to be able to pay fast, so the wallet widget is shown on all main pages, including the hawker centre selection page. So in the event the app is unable to detect the user’s location, payment is still going to be as quick. Users can tap the icon beside wallet balance to show recent transactions.

At the bottom of the top up page, we have the cash top up message. No need to click on a toggle just to see it. For recurring top up with Paylah or NETS, auto top up amount is shown on the toggle.
