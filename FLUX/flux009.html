FLUX #009 - Get

28 February 2020 (First published on 8kguy.com)

Online shopping has become a norm in recent years, and nobody can live without social media these days. What happens when you combine them? That is what the “social buying” app Get hopes to define.

But if you download their app, you will see that they are nowhere there yet. What are they trying to achieve? How does going “social” benefit the user? All of that seems pretty muddy at the moment.

Before I begin with the issues, you need to understand what this app is able to do first. Basically, there are 3 types of things you can sell on the platform: membership, event tickets and merchandise.

Issue #1: A Lack of Focus

The first things you see when you open an app should always be the most commonly used ones. For this app, that should be the store. Unfortunately, that is not the case here.

Two things flood the first screen: toggles to instructions and a live list of what others are buying. Both of these are not the reason why users are on board this app.

Also, why would I want random strangers to know what I am getting? And why would I want to know what they are getting? I know they are trying to make the platform “social”. But even on social media, you only see posts that are relevant to you or from someone you follow.

Otherwise, there isn’t anything very “social” about this app.

Where do you find your purchases? You can access those pages under the “Account” tab. But with so many icons on the same page, all in the same color, identifying them is not easy.

Issue #2: Too Many Categories, Too Few Users

Right now, there are a total of 37 categories of products. And within each category is many more subcategories. With the current adoption rate, it is no surprise that most of them are empty.

These categories are suppose to help users find relevant products. However, when there are so many of them, it only confuses the user. And when the user finds out they are mostly empty, it leads to frustration.

Issue #3: Unnecessarily Different Seller’s UI

Want to set up your own store? Tap on “Seller Center” on the top left corner. Create a new brand, or select a current one you manage. You will be brought to a page that looks totally different.

Welcome to Seller Center! Don’t you feel like you are using another app? The lack of coherence creates a bloated experience. At the same time, first time users may have difficulties leaving the seller’s interface. To switch back, you have to go to settings, and tap the very last option.

Concept by 8K

Your Personalized “News Feed”

Wallet balance is shown at the very top of the page. This gives the user assurance that there is enough money in their account before they make a purchase.

Drawing inspiration from Facebook, I made the home page like a news feed. Users can tap on the star to like the post. You will only see the following types of posts on your news feed:

1) Posts that your friend liked
2) Photos your friends uploaded
3) Recommended posts based on your past transactions and likes

Since liking a post shares it with your friends, there isn’t a need for a share button. To find out more about the post, you can tap on it. Depending on whether it is a merch, event or membership, different information will be shown. Users can then tap on the “Get” button to purchase it.

At the bottom of each post, users can see who liked or purchased the product. Users can also leave a comment for the seller or other users. Alternatively, they can PM the seller via their profile page.

Earlier, I mentioned that users can post images to the platform. To avoid spam, users can only posts images that are taken at events. And to keep the experience simple, there are no photo descriptions. Users can however still comment and like on these photo posts.

Building Seller’s UI into Main Interface

I made the seller’s UI part of the main interface, accessible via the nav bar below. To reduce taps needed, stores you manage are shown with their options in a list. You can swipe left/right to switch store. Sellers can easily tell if they have a new order from the number beside the “orders” option.

Profile Page

Just like on every social media app, every user has his/her own profile page. When you take a look at other people’s profile page, you will be able to see all their posts. Here, you can add them as friend or send them a message. Less used toggles, like reporting a user, are hidden under the “more” toggle.

Chat & Account Page

Under the “Chat” page, you will find 2 things: private messages from other users and comments on your post or a post you commented on. You can message your friends from here by tapping on the “+” icon.

Lastly, we have the “me” page. This is where you can find your purchases, uploads and more.
